When Did Chillies & Spices Become Popular?

Spice History

Spices were first used in Ancient Egypt as food preservatives or for medicines, but spices really became popular in the year 1501, when the spice trade occurred. It all started when Pedro Alvares Cabral brought spices from India to Europe under his command. Since then, people have been taking routes like the spice routes from Europe to Asia to trade gold for spices and vice versa. They also used spices to trade with other things and some countries stole spices to grow them in their own country. That's how spice populartiy around the world began.

Chili History

Chillies were first spread by Christopher Columbus who named it a pepper since that was originally what he wanted to discover or find. He brought it to Spain, naming it a spice. The Portuguese soon got hold of the "spice" (which was actually chillies) and was the first country to have spread the chili around the world under Pedro Alvares Cabral's command. And yes, that was the same man and country that spread spices around the world! Who knew?!
