Glossary

  • Chilli: a small hot-tasting pod of a variety of capsicum, used in sauces, relishes, and spice powders. There are various forms with pods of differing size, colour, and strength of flavour.
  • Spice: an aromatic or pungent vegetable substance used to flavour food, e.g. cloves, pepper, or cumin.
  • Clay Tablet: used as a writing medium in the Ancient Near East
  • Ivory: a hard creamy-white substance composing the main part of the tusks of an elephant, walrus, or narwhal, often (especially formerly) used to make ornaments and other articles.
  • Seasoning: salt, herbs, or spices added to food to enhance the flavour.
  • BC: Before Christ (used to indicate that a date is before the Christian era).
  • Cultivated: refined and well educated.
  • Pungent: having a sharply strong taste or smell
  • Originated: having a specified beginning
  • Sensory Neurons: Neurons that transmit our five senses to our brain
  • Civilisation: the stage of human social and cultural development and organization that is considered most advanced.
  • Preservatives: substances used to preserve foodstuff (a substance used as food, or to make food), wood, or other materials against decay.
  • Tropical: very hot and humid.
  • Mummification: (especially in ancient Egypt) to preserve (a body) by embalming and wrapping it in cloth.
  • Consume: eat, drink, or ingest (food or drink).
  • Scoville Scale: A way to measure how hot a certain food is
