A Chili or Spice?

Many people get confused with chillies and spices, but on this page, you will find out that they are both quite different. Here is what makes a chili and spice different:

Spice Meaning

An aromatic or pungent vegetable substance used to flavour food, e.g. cloves, pepper, or cumin.

Chili Meaning

A small hot-tasting pod of a variety of capsicum, used in sauces, relishes, and spice powders. There are various forms with pods of differing size, colour, and strength of flavour.

  • Similarities
  • They are both used to flavour foods
  • They are also used to flavour drinks
  • They can be consumed cooked or uncooked
  • They both were spread to the world by the same country
  • They both can taste pungent
  • They are both edible

  • Differences
  • Spices can come from lots of different food types like fruits, seeds or plants while chillies are originated from one food group: fruits
  • Not all spices are hot eg: cinnamon, while chillies always activate those sensory neurons in our mouth, making them hot
  • Human civilisation has used chillies longer then thay have used spices
  • Spices are native to India and chillies are native to Mexico
  • The hottest chili is hotter than the hottest spice
